<p><p>The Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT) devices market is primarily categorized into unilateral and bilateral types based on electrode placement. Unilateral ECT targets one hemisphere of the brain, typically resulting in fewer cognitive side effects and a faster recovery, making it suitable for certain patients. In contrast, bilateral ECT engages both hemispheres, often delivering a more robust therapeutic effect, which can be beneficial for severe depression cases. Both types cater to varying patient needs and treatment goals in mental health therapy.</p></p>
