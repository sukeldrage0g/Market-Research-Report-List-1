<p><p>The major orthopedic joint replacement implants market encompasses three primary types: ceramics, metals, and polymers. Ceramic implants are known for their biocompatibility, wear resistance, and low friction, making them ideal for hip and knee replacements. Metal implants, typically made of titanium or cobalt-chromium, offer durability and strength, often used in load-bearing joints. Polymer implants, usually made from polyethylene, provide cushioning and flexibility, playing a critical role in enhancing joint movement and comfort. Each type serves specific patient needs and applications.</p></p>
