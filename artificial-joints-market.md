<p><p>The artificial joints market comprises several types, including ceramics, alloys, and oxinium. Ceramic artificial joints offer excellent wear resistance and biocompatibility, making them suitable for patients with metal allergies. Alloy artificial joints, typically made of titanium or cobalt-chromium, provide strength and durability. Oxinium artificial joints consist of a zirconium alloy coated with a ceramic surface, enhancing wear resistance while reducing friction. Other market segments may include polymer-based joints or innovative composite materials designed to improve performance and longevity in joint replacement surgeries.</p></p>
