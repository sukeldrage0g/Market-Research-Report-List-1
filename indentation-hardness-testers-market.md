<p><p>The indentation hardness testers market comprises various types that measure material hardness based on indentation techniques. Leeb hardness testers utilize rebound principles for portable testing. Brinell testers apply a hardened steel ball under a specific load, while Rockwell testers measure hardness based on depth of indentation. Vickers and Knoop testers use diamond indenters for micro-hardness measurements. Shore testers assess hardness in polymers and elastomers, and Webster testers are designed for softer metals. Other testers may include specialized or less common methods.</p></p>
