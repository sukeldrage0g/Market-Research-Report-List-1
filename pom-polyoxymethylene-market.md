<p><p>The Polyoxymethylene (POM) market is divided into two primary types: POM-H and POM-C. POM-H, also known as homopolymer, offers higher stiffness and strength, making it ideal for demanding applications in automotive and engineering sectors. Conversely, POM-C, or copolymer, is characterized by improved chemical resistance and lower melting points, rendering it suitable for applications requiring higher flexibility and toughness. Both types are widely utilized in manufacturing precision parts, electrical components, and consumer goods, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
