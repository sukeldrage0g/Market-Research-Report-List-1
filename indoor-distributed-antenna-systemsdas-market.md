<p><p>Indoor Distributed Antenna Systems (DAS) enhance cellular connectivity within buildings by distributing signals from a central source through a network of antennas. There are two main types: Active DAS and Passive DAS. Active DAS uses powered components, including amplifiers, to manage and distribute signals, providing greater coverage and capacity. Passive DAS relies solely on passive components like cables and antennas, requiring minimal power but offering limited range and performance. Both systems cater to varying needs for wireless connectivity in indoor environments.</p></p>
